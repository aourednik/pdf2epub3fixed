André
Ourednik
is
a
researcher,
writer
and
lecturer
at
the
Swiss
Federal
Institute
of
Technology
(EPFL)
and
the
University
of
Neuchâtel.
Trained
in
geography,
philosophy
and
the
digital
humanities,
he
is
the
author
of
a
doctoral
thesis
on
algorithmic
modelling
of
the
inhabited
space,
as
well
as
numerous
arti-
cles
in
newspapers
and
specialised
journals
in
geography,
social
science,
and
human-
computer
interaction.
Since
over
ten
years,
he
is
teaching
on
the
concept
of
nature
to
an
interdisciplinary
audience
of
polytechnic
students,
and
has
worked
for
several
years
as
a
data
scientist
for
the
Swiss
Confederation.
His
publications
include
the
hypertext
Wiki-
tractatus
(book,
Hélice
Hélas,

and
digital
creation
1
),
Les
cartes
du

,

Om-
niscience
,

satire
of
the
data
civilisation
(novel,
La

Hypertopie
